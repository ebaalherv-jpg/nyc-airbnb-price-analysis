# NYC Airbnb Price Analysis

Analysis of Airbnb listings across New York City boroughs to understand
how price varies by room type, neighbourhood, and review activity.

## Business Question
How do listing prices and demand differ across NYC boroughs and room
types, and what does that suggest about market positioning?

## The Story

Airbnb in New York isn't one market — it's several overlapping ones, and
treating it as a single market hides where the real opportunity sits.
Breaking listings down by borough, room type, and price shows exactly
where that assumption falls apart.

Hotel-room listings stood out immediately: they average roughly $600 a
night, far above private or shared rooms — a segment operating on an
entirely different pricing logic than the rest of the market. Manhattan,
meanwhile, is where supply concentrates: it holds the highest share of
both "entire home/apt" and "private room" listings of any borough, which
means hosts elsewhere are competing in a much thinner market.

The finding that stood out most: price and review volume don't move
together consistently across boroughs. A listing's popularity isn't a
reliable proxy for what it can charge. For a host or platform strategist,
that's the real insight — pricing power here comes from location and room
type, not popularity.

## Tools
Tableau Public, CSV data (Inside Airbnb)

## Key Findings
- Hotel room listings command the highest average price (~$600),
  well above private rooms and shared rooms.
- Manhattan has the highest concentration of "Entire home/apt" and
  "Private room" listings compared to other boroughs.
- Price and review volume don't move together consistently across
  boroughs, suggesting review count alone isn't a strong price predictor.
